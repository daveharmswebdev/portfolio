Bio

Currently I am an RN, and I love my job, but I love my fellow nurses more. So I want to be able to build an EHR that puts them and all front line staff first, because that’s putting patient care first.

I have three children and four rental properties. I stay busy. And it’s my beautiful wife keeps me balanced.

Working for my mother in her restaurant helped me to be a better landlord. I learned a lot about owning your own business. A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush. If you got a good customer or a good tenant, do what it takes to keep them.

If you have an HVAC problem, hire a pro. A pro will do it faster and remarkably better. Ceiling fan, do that yourself. Electricians are too expensive for small jobs. Same goes for most plumbing and carpentry. Believe it or not, it’s cheaper to have someone else fix your window screens. You’re paying for parts either way, and the it’s cheaper to pay for the labor than it is to buy the tools.

Loved going to University of Texas and getting my English degree. Shakespeare and Hemingway are awesome. Loved my Wilco and Dylan phase. And now that I have children of 11, 8 and 1, I love my Disney phase.

I want to write code that improves someone else’s work life. As an RN the only time IT gets my attention is when it lets me down. I have vivid memories of waiting for a frozen EHR to thaw out while a patient is writhing in pain so I can admit them and get meds ordered. Sure, you can override the system and get meds directly from the dispensery, but it’s not supposed to work like that!

Learning to code is a pleasure. I love the method: the process of writing a line, and refreshing the browser, and making changes. But I also love the finished product, the way cooking produces a meal, the way woodworking produces a bench, the way planting flowers and bushes makes a garden. These are all things to be proud of, things you can look at everyday and revisit.

When my career is over, I want to know that I helped people. And I want to leave behind a good body of work.
